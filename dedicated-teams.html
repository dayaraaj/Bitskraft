    Outsourcing Models
    Dedicated Outsourcing Model
    The dedicated outsourcing model involves hiring a team of remote workers who focus exclusively on your projects. Managed similarly to an in-house team, this approach is ideal for long-term collaborations and ensures high accountability. Your dedicated team works as an extension of your in-house staff, delivering consistent results and adapting to your needs.
    Features
    Exclusive Focus: Team dedicated solely to your projects.
    Management: Managed like an in-house team.
    Best For: Long-term projects with high accountability.
    Time And Material Model
    This model, also known as “T&M,” is used when a company hires a vendor to complete a project based on the actual time and resources used. It’s suitable for projects with many unknowns or evolving requirements. You pay for the actual time spent and materials used, which allows for flexibility in project scope and changes.
    Pros
    Flexiblity: Adapt to changes and new requirements.
    Transparency: Track time and resources used.
    Cons
    Cost Variability: Potential for higher costs than initially budgeted.
    Budgeting Difficulty: Harder to predict final costs.
    Fixed Price Model
    The fixed-price model sets a pre-agreed price for a project, with the vendor responsible for delivering within the specified timeframe and quality. It’s suitable for well-defined projects with clear deliverables and deadlines. This model offers predictable costs but can have limitations if project scopes change.
    Pros
    Predictable: Clear, upfront pricing.
    Reduced Ris: No unexpected cost overruns.
    Cons
    Cost Variability: Harder to accommodate scope changes.
    Budgeting Difficulty: Potential challenges in meeting exact requirements.
